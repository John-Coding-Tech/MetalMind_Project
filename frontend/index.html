        ⚖️
        
Risk > Price

        
The core principle behind every MetalMind recommendation.

      


      

        

          Cheap does not mean good. A supplier offering rock-bottom pricing may
          lack credentials, verifiable contact details, or a track record —
          all signals of real business risk. MetalMind never
          recommends the cheapest supplier blindly.
        


        
How it works

        
    
          
  • Risk score first — each supplier is scored on website quality, certifications, contact info, reviews, country/domain consistency, and suspiciously low pricing (a cheap outlier is itself a risk signal).
    • 
          
  • Value formula — value_score = price_score × (1 − 0.6 × risk_score). Higher risk directly reduces value.
    • 
          
  • Soft cap on high-risk — once risk exceeds a threshold, value is smoothly capped, so a cheap-but-risky supplier cannot outrank a safer, pricier one.
    • 
          
  • Three analysis modes —
            Calculation-Based (pure rule-based scoring),
            AI Analysis (a standalone LLM evaluation of every supplier),
            and AI Comparison (rules and AI run in parallel, reconciled into a green/yellow/red decision tier).
          
    • 
        


        

          💡
          Rule of thumb: If the price looks too good to be true, the risk score will usually confirm it.
